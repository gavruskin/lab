Areas of scientific interest

My research interests are in biomathematics and bioinformatics. I am working on mathematical and computational methods in evolutionary biology, population genetics, epidemiology, and cancer biology. The specific projects I am currently involved in are:
  • Fitness landscapes and gene interactions: models, computing, analysis.
    Collaborators: Niko Beerenwinkel, Kristina Crona, Bernd Sturmfels, and Will Ludington.
  • Gene interactions in genome-scale data.
    Collaborators: Ewa Szczurek and Niko Beerenwinkel.
  • Statistical and computational aspects of phylogenetic time-trees.
    Collaborators: Alexei Drummond, Tanja Stadler, Erick Matsen, and Chris Whidden.
At early stages of my career, I worked on various topics in mathematics and computer science, with computational methods and algorithms being a unifying theme of my research. In mathematics, I have worked in computable model theory, algebra, and analysis. In computer science, I have mostly been working on computational complexity and data structures.
